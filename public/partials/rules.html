General Statement

General statement

Angels of Eureka is dedicated to providing support to bereaved parents and families who are suffering as the result of the death of their child, children, mom, dad, sister, brother grandparent, etc... In no way does this group/website take any responsibility for your mental health. If you are suffering from depression, or if you are having suicidal thoughts, please call 911 or consult your physician immediately. If you see warning signs that point to such by someone on this site, contact Site Administration immediately so we can help guide them.

Groups are available to those seeking uplifting support from other bereaved families walking the same journey. Angels of Eureka believes in showing tolerance, broad-mindedness, patience and acceptance of and towards fellow grieving families and parents, understanding that we are all from different backgrounds, cultures, countries and religions.

To families of lost loved ones your unique grief journey will be shaped by many factors. No two deaths are mourned the same way. Your grief is yours and yours only. Although other bereaved families understand what you are going through, there is no right or wrong way to grieve. We just have to focus on grieving healthy, not only for ourselves but our families. Your grief is what it is. It's your right to express it.

Our purpose is to offer understanding, suggestions for coping, support, friendship, and most of all, I hope for all who are struggling with grief that this journey at Angels of Eureka finds you with moving towards a better tomorrow.

People will be divided in groups. That is why it is important to enter your correct registration information so you are placed in the appropriate group of peers to support you. That doesn't mean someone can't invite you to their group, you can join other groups via an invitation. We make sure you control your audience.

You will be able to have more then one memorial wall if you choose, all within your pages. With many tools!

Your loved one will go on the Memorial Wall of Honor if you share their uploaded photo at the time of registration. You can share photos, videos and many other things in your own Memorial Web Site Wall. We wanted to have a place where you could put everything in one place.

This is a Free Service.

So one that note: Things to say and not say to a grieving person/ parent

Things NOT to Say

Things Not to Say to Someone in Grief:
  • How are you?
  • You'll get through it, be strong.
  • He brought this on himself/ herself, it was his fault.
  • She’s in a better place.
  • Its been a while, aren't you over her/him yet?
  • He lived a long time, at least he didn't die young.
  • God must have wanted her/him there because she was such a good person.
  • You're young. You can always have another child.
  • I know exactly how you feel.
  • I guess it was his/her time to go.
  • Everything happens for a reason, life goes on.

Things TO Say

Things to Say to Someone in Grief:
  • Just call and say I was thinking of you
  • I can't imagine what you're going through.
  • I'm so sorry for your loss.
  • I don't know what to say, I wish I had the right words to comfort you.
  • You, your family and your loved one will be in my thoughts and prayers.
  • She was so nice to me; one of my favorite memories of her was...(share a happy memory of the person who passed)
  • Whenever you want to talk, just know I am a phone call away.
  • She/He was so wonderful, she/ he be missed by so many people.
  • I'm your friend—I'm here for you.
  • If you can't think of anything to say, a hug may be appropriate.
  • Sometimes just be with the person, you don't have to say anything.

Its OK to bring up their loved one, they want you too. They want you to Remember! It shows you care!

Remember, grieving the loss of a loved one is the worst pain someone can endure. Be respectful and polite. Don't discount anyone’s feelings. Even if someone puts on a brave face and looks like he or she is handling it well, don't assume that person is. Show that you care. Actions often speak louder then words. You can just listen and support. Offer to take them to the grocery store, watch the children for an afternoon, and help around the house. These gestures mean a lot to a person whose world has just been turned upside down.

You might ask a person 50 times to do something with you and they keep saying No, Just move on and understand they are not ready, don't force them to feel what they are not ready for. Keep calling and trying because one day they will say yes, and they will know you never gave up or forgot about them.
