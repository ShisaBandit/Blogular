Angels of Eureka is about Life, Love, Hope Support and Comfort for those dealing with tragedy or loss.

Your loved one can be celebrated and remembered here on the Cirle of Life, by creating a Memorial Wall for them. Here on the Circle of Life, we call them your "Angel". Preserve and cherish their legacy and share memories and precious details about your Angel with their close family and friends. You choose what to post. You choose who can participate in conversations. You choose who can view the content that you share.

Memorial Wall Features (Angels or Pets):

  • Angel's Timeline: See all the current topics, photos and events about your Angel in one convenient place. Share a memory for all of his/her friends and family to enjoy.
  • Photos: Share photos, and organize them into galleries. You can also upload your own Imikimi montage creations!
  • Videos:Share videos of your Angel. You add either youtube or Animoto videos!
  • Angel Anniversaries: Specify a date that is important to the legacy of your Angel. Share a memory with all his/her loved ones, and celebrate their life and encourage one another!

We also provide you the ability to create customized, private support groups for whatever cause you may have. Build you network here and grow strong together!

Private Group Features:
  • Group's Timeline: See all the current topics, photos and events about your group in one convenient place. Share a thought for your group network to discuss.
  • Photos: Share photos, and organize them into galleries.
  • Videos:Share videos with your group. You add either youtube or Animoto videos!
  • Group Events: Create and share events for your group.

Signing up is easy. Once you sign-up for your free personal profile, you can create a memorial wall for your lost loved one. On the memorial page you can share memories via photos, videos and celebrate their memory with Angel anniversaries. You can invite your friends to join your private network as well as meet people with experience similar to yours and find and give much needed support and comfort!

The story behind the Angels of Eureka and the Circle of Life

Hello I am one of the owners of Angels of Eureka! I would like to tell you how Angels of Eureka was born! So you know who we are and why we started Angels of Eureka!

 

It was through a horrific tragedy that happened to my family! On Nov 11, 2010, I received a call that changed my family forever! When I picked up the phone all I heard from the person on the other end of the phone line was that my daughter and her children were stuck upstairs in a horrific fire. I lost all of them from this fire physically and emotionally! We buried our 3 beautiful grandchildren 12, 8 and 5 years old. We had no choice but to place the surviving child with his dad after a year and half of no one helping me to get my daughter, who had no insurance the help she needed!

 

You see my daughter died that day in her heart because it was so broken. I will be writing about my Journey in the pages I am introducing to you! I figured out writing is healing. We have walked through heavy waters! With Abuse, Divorce, HUGE loss and drugs!

 

I would love others to share your stories! Our website is very secure! Seems Complicated until you use the tools! You can start creating your own support network here in the website, which we provide to use for free! Our webmasters are working on a series of tutorial videos to help you! Our webmasters will answer any of your questions and help you with any problems..God Blessed me with these two great men that heard my cry!

 

God Has Blessed me a lot on this Journey, and yes I have seen God move from day one of this fire! You see I can't do this without God, He is my light and he is guiding me!

 

I didn't know how to pick up the pieces of my life. I stumbled around numb inside overwhelmed with pain! I couldn't balance all my children as they were falling apart. My daughter got so bad she wanted to be with her children on the other side. You see she died first hearing her children suffocating and cry with fear after the explosion upstairs that knocked her off her feet and separated her and two of her children, one child burned alive!

 

My boys heard it too, while trying to get upstairs but it was to hot! Neighbours outside felt the ground shake after the explosion upstairs and everyone screaming! One of the boys finally felt a hand and drug my daughter's dead body downstairs and my son brought her back to life digging soot out of her nose and throat using the Emergency Medical Skills he learned that previous year!

 

I have watched my daughter suffer with pain and grief 3.5 years not wanting to live, not having a reason to live and not having any passion or hope! How does someone live a normal life for 31 years and after something like this come back?

 

As a mother, I yelled to God, not at God as I found my daughter laying passed out on her children's graves! I could find no one to help me for 1.5 years after the fire because my daughter had too much to say that was the truth! So I learned courage and I gained strength because Love is a very powerful weapon! I went around to attorneys and people knowing my daughter had no insurance seeking help! Because I could not bury my child, I already buried the loves of my life! I couldn't bury anymore of my loves!

 

I ended up writing Intervention TV! They flew out immediately to save my family! I felt like God threw me a life raft! A&E paid for the intervention and flights. A place called The Refuge in Florida gave my daughter a scholarship! The Refuge is one of the Top Trauma Faculties in our Country for PTSD, Survivors Guilt, etc. Our soldiers are sent there! These were my Earth Angels..God sent them to me!

 

I worked in Holistic Health at the time of the fire my friends around the country told me don't worry about the show, look at the help they are bringing.That's what I did! I met the most wonderful people! This was real life! I have never been so afraid in my life!

 

Angels of Eureka supports a place called Onsite because A&E called Onsite and OnSite flew to our families aid. Doctor Phil likes also supports OnSite.They came with a team to help us with a workshop of trauma therapy!

 

Miles the CEO changed my life the minute he walked in the door. He let me know he was there to help me and my family but it took me to embrace that help! It took me awhile but I have learned it well! Miles showed me my life still Mattered. I remember Miles pulling up a chair in front of me, looking in my eyes and saying I love you! Very Powerful Words!

 

In Fact he said, he wouldn't be surprised if I became a Therapist, LOL! All I can say is I have walked through it!  I learned it through the worse experience in my life with the tools that were gifted to me from all the kind people that rescued me and my family!

 

The Refuge told us not to take my daughter back to Texas her survival would be better to start away from all of the trauma that happened before and after the fire! My husband retired and within a week of the Therapist telling our daughter that. Our Family landed in Arkansas because we have some precious family here! But we have meet some good people here, that tried to help us and catch us! I am grateful, Thank you!

 

My daughter wanted a shop, So with the families help. We created a shop and called it Angels of Eureka, not knowing where it was going to carry us! So many people touched our lives we wanted to give back. I didn't know where I was going, I felt very lost but I do know more now because I have a dream!

To Bereaved Parents & Grandparents

A bereaved parent understands the pain that the death of a child brings. Grandparents can suffer that same pain because of it being their grandchild and child hurting. I think its important Grandparents understand what their child is going through and the best educators are the parents.

A Death of a Loved one is part of life as we were to understand, ''unless they were gone to soon''. Which in most cases people can get through the grief up to few years with the right support and counseling.

A death of a child is lifelong, it changes a person forever, they can lose their identities. They feel very alone in their Journeys, They learn to wear a mask, Some go towards alcohol, pills, drugs, We will not Judge them for you have not lived what they went through, We will welcome them to speak freely so they know its OK and really its normal if they don't have tools to cope.

Some feel very suicidal and if you see any suicidal behavior please contact admin. we would like to help them and reach out to them as soon as possible.

We are all traveling the journey of grief and even though our grief is our own, the understanding, support and compassion you receive from another grieving parent / grandparent is of utmost importance.

Grief is what we think and feel on the inside when someone we love dies. It's numbness, sadness, anger, guilt, and sometimes relief, all rolled up into one. It's the pain in our gut and a hole in our heart. Grief can be physical pain as well, we can have a lot of downs in the beginning process so be gentle to yourself. With support from people that have walked in your shoes, they can help you through..

Mourning is expressing our grief, letting it out somehow.
We mourn when we cry, talk about death, write about it, punch a pillow or scream at the top of our lungs.

Everyone grieves inside when the child that was loved with such passion dies. We MUST mourn to begin the healing process and to learn to live a much different life now.

Every parent is welcome here no matter how you have lost your child.

In no way does this group take any responsibility for your mental health. If you are suffering from depression or if you are having suicidal thoughts, please call 911 or consult your physician.

We all know one of the signs of grief is anger there are times when we are angry and lash out but lets not try to lash out to the other grieving person. Hopefully we will get through those times together. We all look for a way to connect with our lost children or loved ones. Everyone has their own way of trying to do this. For some it might be through religion, for others through spiritualism, yet others might not have found any way. Whatever way you choose, its YOUR way. Please respect that we all have our own way, and don't judge. Please don't think that your way is better than anyone else's, its just different. We have to accept that this is how we are.

Things Not to Say to Someone in Grief:
  • How are you?
  • You'll get through it, be strong.
  • He brought this on himself/ herself, it was his fault.
  • She’s in a better place.
  • Its been a while, aren't you over her/him yet?
  • He lived a long time, at least he didn't die young.
  • God must have wanted her/him there because she was such a good person.
  • You're young. You can always have another child.
  • I know exactly how you feel.
  • I guess it was his/her time to go.
  • Everything happens for a reason, life goes on.
Things to Say to Someone in Grief:
  • Just call and say I was thinking of you
  • I can't imagine what you're going through.
  • I'm so sorry for your loss.
  • I don't know what to say, I wish I had the right words to comfort you.
  • You, your family and your loved one will be in my thoughts and prayers.
  • She was so nice to me; one of my favorite memories of her was...(share a happy memory of the person who passed)
  • Whenever you want to talk, just know I am a phone call away.
  • She/He was so wonderful, she/ he be missed by so many people.
  • I'm your friend—I'm here for you.
  • If you can't think of anything to say, a hug may be appropriate.
  • Sometimes just be with the person, you don't have to say anything.

Its OK to bring up their loved one, they want you too. They want you to Remember! It shows you care!

Remember, grieving the loss of a loved one is the worst pain someone can endure. Be respectful and polite. Don't discount anyone’s feelings. Even if someone puts on a brave face and looks like he or she is handling it well, don't assume that person is. Show that you care. Actions often speak louder then words. You can just listen and support. Offer to take them to the grocery store, watch the children for an afternoon, and help around the house. These gestures mean a lot to a person whose world has just been turned upside down.

You might ask a person 50 times to do something with you and they keep saying No, Just move on and understand they are not ready, don't force them to feel what they are not ready for. Keep calling and trying because one day they will say yes, and they will know you never gave up or forgot about them.

Hopefully one day I can incorporate a group for NA and AA here to help those who couldn't cope with losing a loved one because they didn't have the tools so they have sponsor support. We will grow as we go along this Journey, We are just getting started. Any Ideas are welcomed here at Angels of Eureka!

Thank you for taking the time to read our rules, we hope you have a wonderful journey here and we bring some hope for your tomorrow!
